Figure: Group Average Angular Displacement During the Vibration Phase (Illusory Drift).

This figure contrasts the independent physical displacement of both arms during the kinesthetic illusion stimulation phase (N=7 participants). 

The x-axis represents time in seconds aligned exactly to the onset of the vibration motor (T=0). The y-axis displays the absolute physical displacement from the participant's baseline orientation at T=0 in degrees. 

The blue line represents the Manipulated Arm (Sensor 1), which is shown to remain physically stationary near 0 displacement throughout the trial. 
The red line represents the Subject Matching Arm (Sensor 2), which exhibits progressive physical drift as participants reflexively tracked the illusory subjective movement of their manipulated arm. 

Solid lines denote the respective group means, and the shaded regions represent the boundaries of the standard error of the mean (SEM).
